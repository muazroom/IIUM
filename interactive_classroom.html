📝 Assessment

Assessment helps us know what students have learned. There are many types. Explore them below.

🎯 Formative Assessment (During Learning) ▼

These happen while teaching. They help the teacher adjust.

  • Thumbs up / thumbs down check
  • Exit tickets (write one sentence)
  • Short quizzes with instant feedback
  • Observing group work
🏁 Summative Assessment (After Learning) ▼

These happen at the end of a unit. They measure overall understanding.

  • Final exams
  • Projects and portfolios
  • Standardized tests
✅ Authentic Assessment (Real-Life Tasks) ▼

Students do a real task, not just answer multiple-choice questions.

  • Presenting a Hajj brochure
  • Creating a video about wudu steps
  • Writing a letter of advice using Islamic values

📖 The Inimitability of the Qur'an (I'jaz)

The Qur'an is miraculous and cannot be copied. Explore the four main types of its inimitability.

1. 🗣️ Linguistic & Literary I'jaz ▼

The Qur'an uses Arabic in a way that is unique, beautiful, and impossible to match.

  • Perfect rhythm and rhyme without losing meaning.
  • Words chosen with extreme precision.
  • Arabs at the time were masters of poetry, yet they could not produce one chapter like it.

Challenge: "And if you are in doubt about what We have sent down upon Our Servant, then produce a chapter the like thereof…" (2:23)

2. 🔬 Scientific I'jaz ▼

The Qur'an contains facts that match modern science, revealed long before they were discovered.

Embryology

Stages of human creation described in detail (23:14).

Universe

Expansion of the universe mentioned (51:47).

3. ⚖️ Legislative I'jaz ▼

The laws in the Qur'an are just and balanced for all times.

  • Family law protects women, orphans, and the weak.
  • Economic rules forbid exploitation and interest (riba).
  • Criminal law aims at justice, not revenge.
4. 🔮 Prophetic I'jaz ▼

The Qur'an tells about future events that came true.

  • Victory of the Romans over Persians after defeat (30:2-4).
  • Preservation of the Qur'an itself for all time.
